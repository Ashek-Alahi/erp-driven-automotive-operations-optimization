MP-0: ERP Optimization in Automobile Industry

Master’s Project Report (MSc Information Technology – ERP Specialization)

Overview
This repository contains my Master’s Project (MP-0) completed as part of the MSc in Information Technology at Kyoto College of Graduate Studies for Informatics.

The study analyzes how Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) systems improve operational efficiency in the automobile industry, focusing on integration across production, supply chain, inventory, finance, and customer management.

 Research Objective
To evaluate how ERP systems optimize automobile industry operations through:

Production planning & automation
Supply chain integration
Inventory control & demand forecasting
Financial and resource optimization
Customer relationship management (CRM) improvement

 Industry Focus
Automobile manufacturing operations with ERP-driven digital transformation.

Key Insights
ERP improves real-time decision-making across departments
Strong reduction in operational inefficiencies and delays
Better coordination between supply chain, production, and sales
AI/IoT integration is the future direction of ERP systems
Implementation challenges include cost, resistance, and integration complexity
 Methodology
Literature review
Case study analysis (Ford, BMW, Toyota, GM)
Qualitative research approach
Industry-based conceptual modeling
